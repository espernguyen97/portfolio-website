5 VIETNAMESE HOT POT
YOU MIGHT NEVER HAVE TRIED



Hot Pot is a best choice when you're craving for something hot and spicy that will warm you up . If you are a Hot Pot Pro, you cannot ignore these top five Vietnamese Hot Pot which are elaborately and nicely decorated and prepared.
Here are the Best 5 Hot Pot in Vietnam: .

1. Lẩu Bò (Vietnamese Beef Hot Pot)
2. Lẩu Thả (“Drop” Hot pot)
3. Lẩu Mắm (Seafood Hot Pot W/ Fermented Fish )
4. Lẩu Riêu Cua Đồng (Crab Hot pot)
5. Lẩu Cá Đuối (Stingray Hot Pot)


Lẩu Bò (Vietnamese Beef Hot Pot)

Lẩu Bò is one of Vietnam's popular Hot Pot and it is available almost everywhere in the country. However, this type of hot pot is even more famous in Da Lat, where the famous Ba Toa beef hot pot brand is located. Beef Hot Pot is such a communal, social meal and a delicious dish which is rich in nutritional value. Vietnamese Beef Hot Pot must be cooked from beef, beef bones, and then added familiar spices such as lemongrass, chili, satay. When eating beef hot pot, you can feel the full sweetness from the bone broth, the light fat taste of young tofu as well as the rich, aromatic sauce. This Hot Pot is usually served with egg noodles or rice noodles. You can also add vegetables such as water spinach, cabbage, mushrooms,etc., and taro slices, young tofu, tofu skin in the hot pot. The feeling of dipping sweet and tender beef into a rich bowl of chao (a special dipping sauce made from fermented bean curd), adding a little green vegetable and then slowly chewing it in the mouth is always what makes "hot pot fans" dying for.

Lẩu Thả (“Drop” Hot Pot)

For many people, Lẩu Thả (“Drop” Hot pot) is probably still a new dish. It’s actually a specialty of Phan Thiet , the capital of Bình Thuận Province on the southeast coast in Vietnam. This hot pot originates from the philosophy of yin and yang in the 5 elements. Lẩu Thả with 5 colors (white, blue, black, red, yellow) corresponding to the taste of spicy, sour, salty, bitter and sweet represents the 5 traditional elements Metal, Wood, Water, Fire and Earth. When traveling to Phan Thiet, you definitely have to try this specialty and feel the delicious taste as well as the elaborate layout of this hot pot. The ingredients of the hot pot include deep herring (fish), different kinds of vegetables, vermicelli, boiled meat, sliced eggs . Each ingredient are neatly placed in a petal of banana flower. Lẩu Thả Thả is often served with vermicelli . When eating, you will "drop" each ingredient placed in the petal of banana flower into the hot pot. The whole process of preparing, processing and decorating this hot pot is quite complicated. But once tasting the sweetness of fish, the fatty taste of eggs and the fresh green vegetables in the hot pot , you will find all the preparation is worthy.

Lẩu Mắm (Seafood Hot Pot W/ Fermented Fish )

If you have a trip to the southern of Vietnam, do not miss the famous hot pot of this region. Lẩu Mắm the combination of the broth cooked with fermented fish paste with seafood, meat and different kinds of spices as well as vegetables from the West of Vietnam such as keo neo (limnocharis flava), sung flower (Nymphaea lotus), banana flower, eggplant, water lily stem, yellow velvetleaf, pumpkin flower, greens, winged bean, sesbania grandiflora flower . The regional fish sauce serves as the hot pot's soul. Southerners frequently pick carefully brewed fish sauce while preparing a tasty hot pot. Young linh fish (mustached danio) or sac fish (snakeskin gourami) , caught during the flood season, are commonly used to make the best fish sauce. The broth of the dish is made of fermented fish paste of the fish sauce. Sugar, minced lemongrass, and green onion are used as seasonings. To further improve the flavor of the hot pot, shrimps, squids, eel and roasted pork are added. The fermented fish paste gives the broth its salty flavour while coconut water add the sweetness to the hot pot. This hot can also be served with a variety of fish from the Mekong Delta, including red snapper, snakehead fish, or basa fish . Chili and lemongrass are essential in this hot pot because they help eliminate the smell of the river fish. In addition, the proportion of fermented fish paste and water must be equal to maintain the broth's balanced flavour and prevent it from becoming overly salty.

Lẩu Riêu Cua Đồng (Paddy Crab Hot Pot)

Going to the city of red phoenix flowers – Hải Phong, you will be treated Lẩu Riêu Cua Đồng - a very special paddy crab hot pot. The main ingredient in this dish is the rice field crabs , rather than the luxurious sea crabs or any other seafood. Rice field crab is a a well-known ingredient since many Vietnamese farmers made their living by haphazardly catching crabs in fields, ponds, or rivers in the past. Hot pot's flavours, which are mild yet well-balanced with crab flavour, not overly greasy but slightly sour and sweet, are perhaps what make it so appealing. Traditionally, pork bones are used to make the broth (pork bone is boiled for many hours so that the broth is naturally sweet). The crab paste (riêu) melts as soon as it reaches your tongue, but the crab flavour would last until you finished the dish. The crab combination (riêu) is sautéed with tomatoes , adding to a subtle sour and sweetness of this hot pot. Paddy Crab Hot Pot be served with Hai Phong special rice paper, fried tofu, beef and water spinach,etc.

Lẩu Cá Đuối (Stingray Hot Pot)

When it comes to Vietnamese hot pot, you cannot forget Lẩu Cá Đuối (Stingray Hot Pot) . This hot pot, which is a famous dish of the coastal city Vung Tau , combines delicious fresh stingray with extremely sweet and sour hot pot. Today, stingray hot pot has spread to many places in Vietnam and becomes a hot pot that is loved by many people. Stingray Hot Pot attracts people because of the fresh ingredients along with the equal proportion of sweet, fatty and firm of Stingray meat in Vung Tau. In particular, the fish bones are soft like cartilage, easy to eat and good for health. Usually, stingray hot pot is served with vermicelli, raw vegetables such as water spinach, banana flowers, mint, etc.
